Venice

Venice, the capital of northern Italy’s Veneto region, is built on more than 100 small islands in a lagoon in the Adriatic Sea. It has no roads, just canals – including the Grand Canal thoroughfare – lined with Renaissance and Gothic palaces. The central square, Piazza San Marco, contains St. Mark’s Basilica, which is tiled with Byzantine mosaics, and the Campanile bell tower offering views of the city’s red roofs.

  • Rialto Bridge
  • St. Mark's Square
  • Grand Canal
  • Bridge of Sighs

Aurora Borealis

An Aurora, also commonly known as the 'Polar Lights' or even the 'Northern Lights', is a natural light display in Earth's sky, predominantly seen in high-latitude regions (around the Arctic and Antarctic. Auroras display dynamic patterns of brilliant lights that appear as curtains, rays, spirals, or dynamic flickers covering the entire sky.

  • Tromso, Norway
  • Swedish Lapland
  • Reykjavik, Iceland
  • Yukon, Canada

Oia, Santorini

Oia is a coastal town on the northwestern tip of Santorini, a Greek Aegean island. The town has whitewashed houses carved into the rugged clifftops, and overlooks a vast caldera filled with water. In a 19th-century mansion, the Naval Maritime Museum has exhibits on local seafaring history, including old figureheads, sailors’ chests and models of old ships. Nearby is the ruined Oia Castle, known for its sunset views.

  • Castle of Oia
  • Blue Domed Church Santorini
  • Ammoudi Bay
  • Domaine Sigalas

Great Barrier Reef

The Great Barrier Reef, located in Australia, is the world's largest coral reef system composed of over 2,900 individual reefs and 900 islands stretching for over 2,300 kilometres over an area of approximately 344,400 square kilometres.

  1. It is the world's largest reef system
  2. It is home to an incredible diversity of species
  3. Corals are alive!!
  4. A bleached reef isn't always a dead reef
